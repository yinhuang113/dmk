In Louisiana, we know how important the waters off our coast are -- to our storm protection, to our way of life, and to our economic freedom.

That's why I recently introduced legislation that would extend our state's coastal boundaries 10 miles into the Gulf of Mexico, instead of the current three miles. Currently, Texas and the East Coast of Florida have the jurisdiction equivalent of 10 miles off their coasts. The legislation that Sen. Vitter and I introduced would give the same rights and privileges to Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, all of Florida, Georgia, South Carolina, North Carolina and Virginia.

This policy change would benefit our commercial and recreational fishermen, particularly for red snapper.

There have been numerous instances where the federal government miscounted fish populations in the Gulf. Those errors impact how long fishing seasons last every year, the success of commercial fishermen, and the enjoyment of recreational fishermen.
